About

I'm Joshua Bostic, a character artist based in Brooklyn, NY. I work across the full range — realistic anatomy to high stylization — with a focused eye for strong silhouette, expressive design, and cultural authenticity.

My characters pull from street culture, animation, and manga, and I bring that same specificity to IP work. I can adapt existing 2D designs into production-ready 3D, build original characters from scratch, or push into creature and grotesque territory when a project calls for it.

Available for full-time roles and freelance contracts at game studios, animation studios, and VFX houses.

What I Bring

01

Stylized & Realistic Range

Most character artists live in one lane. My work spans grounded anatomy to full stylization — useful for studios whose pipelines demand both.

02

2D-to-3D Adaptation

I can take a flat cartoon or manga design and translate its intent into a convincing 3D form without losing what makes the character work.

03

Expressive Character Design

Strong silhouettes, readable from a distance. Creature, grotesque, and villain characters are a specialty — not an afterthought.

04

Clothing & Material Detail

Fabric folds, layered streetwear, tactical gear, soft goods — I treat clothing as a character design tool, not just covering geometry.

05

Street & Urban Aesthetic

A specific cultural lens that most 3D portfolios don't have. Brings authenticity to characters drawn from hip-hop, street fashion, and urban life.

06

Pipeline Awareness

Sculpting is step one. I build with retopo, UVs, texturing, and engine delivery in mind from the start — not as an afterthought at the end.

Services

01

Character Modeling

Full-body character sculpts from concept or reference — human, humanoid, stylized, or realistic. Delivered game-ready or film-ready with clean topology.

02

Creature Design

Non-humanoid characters, monsters, and stylized creatures. Strong anatomical foundation adapted to fantastical and original forms.

03

Full Pipeline

Sculpt → retopology → UV → texturing → rigging → UE5 integration. One artist, end to end.
